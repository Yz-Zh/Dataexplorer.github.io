Story about This Website

FOR INTERNATIONAL STUDENTS

This website integrates the data related to renting in different areas in London. Students can use the classified information to filter suitable areas for renting and view in the map section.

Many international students have rental needs. However, most international students do not know the specific situation of the city where the school is located. At the same time, there are problems with incomplete information on the existing rental websites for international students, such as lack of housing information, inaccurate positioning, and lack of surrounding information. This causes information mismatch and screening difficulties for international students when renting a house.






+ Spatial Scale


In this analysis, we focused on the middle layer super output area (MSOA) in London. MSOA is a geographic hierarchy used to improve the reporting of small area statistics in England and Wales.

The MSOA in London can provide a summary of demographic and related data for each small area. The average population of an MSOA in London in 2010 was 8,346 and there are 983 MSOA in London totally. The sections below will demonstrate the rental markets in London and our analysis about the factors that people may consider when renting a house or flat.

SURVEY

We used a questionnaire to investigate the needs of international students for renting and the surrounding environment, and thus determine the relevant factors affecting the renting of international students. The sample comes from 74 Chinese students who need to rent a house in the UK or have rented a house in the UK. In particular, in the option of related factors that may affect renting, we set 11 possible factors and gave an option of 0 - 5 (the least important - the most important). The result shows that six factors are higher than 3.50 points: Rent, Property type, Distance from universities, Crime rates, No. of restaurants/shops and Transportation, respectively. In addition, the factor of crime rates is the most concerning factor. Therefore, we conducted a series of investigations and analyses around these six important factors in the following sections.

TRANSPORTATION

Public Transport Accessibility Levels (PTAL) is a detailed and accurate measure of the accessibility of a point to the public transport network, taking into account walk access time and service availability. Each area is graded between 0 and 6b, where a score of 0 is very poor access to public transport, and 6b is excellent access to public transport. We will use PTAL in 2015 as the measure of transportation.

+ Grouping of Rental Information

In order to identify MSOAs with similar characteristics and enable users to quickly locate suitable areas for renting, we use clustering to group MSOAs with different renting related information. Combining the results of the questionnaire and the type of data, we use the three variables of rent, crime rate and traffic accessibility (PTAI) for clustering. The clustering results show six groups with similar characteristics:

0: Medium price, high crime rate and medium to low accessibility The MSOAs of this cluster are randomly distributed in the areas of London from the centre to the edge. In particular, compared with other clusters, this cluster has the highest crime rate and relatively low traffic accessibility.

1: Medium price, medium to low crime rate and medium accessibility The cluster is distributed in some MSOAs around the city centre of London. Compared with other areas, these areas have high rental prices, but also have relatively low crime rates and moderate traffic accessibility.

2: Medium to high price, medium crime rate and medium to low accessibility The MSOAs of this cluster are also randomly distributed in the areas of London from the centre to the edge. But compared with cluster 0, this cluster has higher rental prices and lower crime rates. In addition, because the geographical distribution of this cluster is roughly the same as that of cluster 0, its traffic accessibility is slightly lower than that of cluster 0 but the gap is not large.

3: Medium price, low crime rate and very high accessibility The cluster is located in the centre of London, which has relatively high housing prices and the lowest crime rate. Also, because it is located at a traffic hub, traffic accessibility is the highest.

4: Medium to low price, medium to high crime rate and medium to low accessibility This cluster is also distributed in some MSOAs around the city centre of London. But compared with cluster 1, this cluster has lower rents, higher crime rates and lower traffic accessibility.

5: High price, medium crime rate and very low accessibility Most of the MSOAs in this cluster are located on the fringe of London. This cluster has the highest average rental price. However, due to its long distance from the city centre and few public transportation lines, it has the lowest traffic accessibility
